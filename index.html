EMOD: AN EFFICIENT APPROACH FOR LOW RESOURCE CONTROLLABLE EMOTIONAL SPEECH SYNTHESIS

Emotion Intensity Control

Emotion intensity defines how strongly an emotion is expressed in speech. Lower values (0.5) sound softer and more subtle, while higher values (1.0) sound stronger and more expressive. In this system, emotional style is controlled through learned embeddings, which capture how different emotions vary in tone and delivery. Even for low-resource Indian languages, where large annotated emotional datasets are limited, the model adapts using zero-shot learning, meaning it generalizes emotion control without requiring explicit training data for each language.
